Field Manual  /  Heritage

Sixty years from a Houston welding shop.

Sumner Manufacturing was founded in 1965 by Joe Sumner in Houston, Texas. The first products were pipe stands and fit-up tools for the local welding and mechanical-contracting trade. The material lifts came later. The company is now part of B&B SUMNER under the SFE Group umbrella, but the design DNA — rugged, technician-serviceable, made for the bench — runs through every product still in the catalog.

Timeline

Sixty years on the bench.

  • 1965

    Founded in Houston, Texas

    Joe Sumner founds Sumner Manufacturing Company in Houston. The first products are heavy-duty pipe stands and fit-up tools serving the Gulf Coast welding and mechanical-contracting trade — a market dense with refineries, chemical plants, and shipyards.

  • 1970s

    The ST-801 Hi Jack becomes the standard

    The ST-801's 2,500 lb capacity, 1-1/2" Acme thread, and patented Fall Guard safety zone make it a category-defining product. Pipefitters across the U.S. learn to call any heavy-duty pipe stand a "Sumner."

  • 1980s

    Material lifts join the catalog

    Sumner extends into hand-cranked material lifts — the Series 2000 family — recognising that the same trades buying pipe stands also buy lifts to set HVAC equipment and overhead piping. The lifts share Sumner's design ethos: ruggedness over weight, reparability over disposability.

  • 1990s – 2000s

    Series 2100, 2300, 2400, Eventer, Roust-A-Bout

    The line expands into the trade-specific variants documented in this manual. Each new series targets a specific use case: drywall (2300), van-friendly compact (2400), entertainment (Eventer), heavy industrial (Roust-A-Bout). Internal cable routing and tool-free assembly become defining features.

  • 2010s

    Global distribution

    Sumner products distribute in 50+ countries, with offices in Houston, Canada, China, the United Kingdom, and the Netherlands. The MX winch replaces the legacy GH-5T as the standard hand-crank winch across the lift line.

  • 2025

    Acquired by Specialized Fabrication Equipment Group

    In early 2025, Sumner Manufacturing is acquired by Specialized Fabrication Equipment (SFE Group) and subsequently merged with B&B Pipe & Industrial Tools to form B&B SUMNER. The product line continues; serial numbers from the legacy Sumner production are still serviceable using the parts catalog in this manual.

  • 2026

    The catalog today

    B&B SUMNER continues production of the Series 2000, 2100, 2300, 2400, Eventer, and Roust-A-Bout material lifts alongside the original pipe-handling and fit-up product line. This field manual is independent of B&B SUMNER, intended as a vendor-neutral educational reference for the technicians who service their lifts.

Design DNA

Five threads that run through every Sumner product.

01

Bench-serviceable

Sumner products are designed to be repaired by the technician, not returned to the factory. The MX winch is the rare exception — almost everything else can be serviced with hand tools.

02

Hand-powered first

Almost the entire material-lift line is hand-cranked. No batteries, no motors, no electronics — the failure modes are mechanical and visible, and the lift is operable with no infrastructure beyond a flat floor.

03

Trade-specific variants

Rather than one universal lift, Sumner builds variants tuned to a specific trade's loads, reach, and footprint. The 99 lb Series 2300 for solo drywall installers and the 1,500 lb Roust-A-Bout for industrial pipe both share the Sumner platform DNA.

04

Tool-free assembly

The Series 2100 and 2400 contractor lifts assemble in seconds with no tools — a feature that emerged from feedback from technicians who set up and break down a lift multiple times a shift.

05

Long product life

Twenty- and thirty-year-old Sumner lifts are still in routine service. Parts compatibility is broad and well-documented. This is why a manual like this one is useful: the installed base of Sumner lifts spans decades of production.

06

Houston roots

Designed for the trades that built Houston's petrochemical industry — pipefitters, welders, mechanical contractors. The aesthetic and the engineering both reflect that origin: practical, robust, and unapologetically industrial.

About This Manual

Who, why, and how to contribute.

Why this manual exists

The Sumner Lift Field Manual is an independent educational resource for technicians who service Sumner material lifts. It was built because OEM operator's manuals are split across PDFs, distributors' parts pages are scattered across vendor catalogs, and technicians on the bench needed a single integrated reference.

Everything in this manual is summarised from publicly available Sumner Manufacturing literature, distributor catalogs (B&B SUMNER, Sumner Outlet, Toolup, EquipSupply, Energized Engines, Conklin Metal, Airgas), and ASME / OSHA wire-rope inspection standards.

Who this is for

Anyone who picks up a Sumner lift and needs to make a service decision. That includes:

  • Equipment-rental shop technicians
  • HVAC and mechanical-contracting service techs
  • Theatrical / AV touring crews servicing Eventer lifts
  • Industrial maintenance teams running Roust-A-Bouts
  • Apprentices learning the platform from scratch
  • Safety officers building inspection programs
Always verify against the OEM manual Specifications, part numbers, and procedures are summarised here for educational use. For any specific lift, always verify against the operator's manual matched to the lift's serial number and use only Sumner-supplied replacement parts on safety-critical components (cable, winch, sheave, fork carriage, outrigger latches).

Editorial corrections log — 2026.05

This edition incorporates substantial corrections to earlier drafts after a second-pass fact-check against primary sources. Notable changes:

  • Series 2000 capacities corrected — capacity decreases as height increases. 2010 = 1,000 lb · 2015 = 800 lb · 2020 = 800 lb · 2025 = 650 lb (earlier draft had the gradient inverted).
  • Series 2400 capacity split clarified — 2412 = 400 lb, 2416 = 450 lb (earlier draft listed a series-wide 450 lb).
  • Eventer model lineup expanded and corrected — Eventer 16 (500 lb) added; Eventer 20 = 800 lb, Eventer 25 = 650 lb (earlier draft had Eventer 25 = 800 lb).
  • Roust-A-Bout doorway-clearance claim moved to R-100 — only the R-100 (32″ × 40″ base) fits a standard residential doorway; the R-150 and R-250 share a 40″ × 40″ base.
  • Winch designations clarified — Sumner uses two winch generations: the legacy GH-5T (PN 785912, paired with 1/8″ cable PN 774206) and the current MX winch (PN 783590, paired with 7/32″ cable). Both are real Sumner part numbers; they are NOT interchangeable.
  • Cable diameter corrected — current Sumner contractor lifts (2118 and Series 2000) use 7/32″ (5.5 mm) cable, not 1/8″.
  • Minimum drum-wraps spec is per-series — Sumner's Series 2100 manual specifies 4 wraps; Series 2400 manual specifies 3 wraps. Earlier draft incorrectly stated 4 wraps as a universal Sumner spec.
  • Cable-construction claim (7×19) softened — 7×19 is industry-standard for this diameter range, but Sumner's customer-facing copy does not specify the construction. Earlier draft attributed the construction to Sumner directly.
  • "Asbestos-free fiber" brake-disc claim removed — could not be confirmed against any Sumner-published document.
  • Acquisition date hedged — “early 2025” rather than “March 2025” pending direct press-release confirmation.
  • Placeholder part numbers labeled — entries with identifiers prefixed ref: are organizational labels used by this manual, not OEM SKUs. Always cross-check against B&B SUMNER customer service before ordering.
