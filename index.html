The Family

One platform, six purpose-built siblings.

Sumner has been building hand-cranked material lifts in Houston since 1965. The platform is consistent — telescoping aluminum mast, hand-cranked winch, reversible forks — but each series is sized and detailed for a different trade.

Series 2000

Material Lift

Models 2010 / 2015 / 2020 / 2025. Workhorse of the line — 650–1,000 lb capacity, 10 to 25 ft of lift, the lift you'll see most often.

  • Capacity650 – 1,000 lb
  • Lift Height10 – 25 ft
  • TradeGeneral / HVAC
Series 2100

Contractor Lift

Models 2112 / 2118 / 2124. 650 lb, 12–24 ft. Internal cable routing, tool-free assembly, and outriggers on the 2118 / 2124.

  • Capacity650 lb
  • Lift Height12 / 18 / 24 ft
  • TradeContractor / HVAC
Series 2300

Drywall / HVAC Lift

The featherweight — 99 lb body, 150 lb capacity, 65° tilting head. Designed for a single drywall installer working overhead alone.

  • Capacity150 lb
  • Lift Height11 ft 2¾" – 14 ft 9¾"
  • TradeDrywall / HVAC solo
Series 2400

Compact Contractor Lift

Models 2412 / 2416. 450 lb capacity, 12 / 16 ft lift. Van-friendly footprint with 360° cable protection and tool-free setup.

  • Capacity450 lb
  • Lift Height12 / 16 ft
  • TradeCompact / Indoor
Eventer Series

Stage / AV Lift

Eventer 20 & 25. Black-anodized aluminum mast disappears on stage. 500–800 lb, 16–25 ft. Designed for trusses, line array, and lighting.

  • Capacity500 – 800 lb
  • Lift Height16 – 25 ft
  • TradeEntertainment / AV
Roust-A-Bout

Heavy Hitter

R-150 / R-250. 1,500 lb capacity, dual-winch architecture (load winch + mast winch), 4-speed winch, 8" casters. Fits through a standard door.

  • Capacity1,500 lb
  • Lift Height15 / 25 ft
  • TradePipe / Industrial

Anatomy

Five subsystems, one lift.

Almost every Sumner lift in the field decomposes into the same five subsystems. Master these and you can service any model the moment it rolls into your shop.

  1. Cable Sheave / Pulley

    Top-of-mast pulley(s) that route the lift cable. Cracked sheaves, worn flanges, or seized bearings will fray cable in hours.

  2. Carriage & Reversible Forks

    Travels on the inner mast. Forks invert to lift loads from the floor or from above the carriage; check for bent tines and worn rollers.

  3. Hand-Crank Winch

    Self-locking worm-gear or ratchet brake winch (Sumner MX, GH-5T, or vendor-supplied). Single point of failure for raise / hold / lower.

  4. Telescoping Mast

    Two- to four-stage extruded aluminum mast with internal cable routing. Inspect for sidewall dents and verify both stages slide freely.

  5. Base & Outriggers

    Wheeled base (typically 5" locking casters), fold-out stabilizer legs, and ground-engagement feet. The lift is only stable when these are deployed correctly.
