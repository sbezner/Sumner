Field Manual  /  Tools & Torque

The right tool keeps the lift safe.

Sumner lifts are simple machines — you can complete every procedure in this manual with the contents of a midsize service tool roll. But three tools (wire-rope cutter, calibrated torque wrench, swage tool) are non-negotiable, and using the wrong substitute is the most common cause of repeat failures.

Torque Reference

Standard fastener torque values.

Sumner's service literature does not publish a comprehensive torque table for every fastener on every lift. Use the values below for general assembly — always defer to a Sumner-published value where one exists for safety-critical fasteners.

Size (UNC) Grade 5 · Dry Grade 5 · Lubricated Grade 8 · Dry Grade 8 · Lubricated
1/4"-20 8 ft-lb 6 ft-lb 12 ft-lb 9 ft-lb
5/16"-18 17 ft-lb 13 ft-lb 24 ft-lb 18 ft-lb
3/8"-16 30 ft-lb 23 ft-lb 45 ft-lb 35 ft-lb
7/16"-14 50 ft-lb 35 ft-lb 70 ft-lb 50 ft-lb
1/2"-13 75 ft-lb 55 ft-lb 105 ft-lb 80 ft-lb
Dry vs. lubricated "Dry" = clean, plain (uncoated/unlubricated) threads. "Lubricated" = oil, anti-seize, or thread-locker on threads. Lubrication reduces friction, so torque must drop ~25–30% to reach the same clamp load. Using a dry value on lubricated threads will overload — and possibly snap — the fastener.
Identifying grade Grade 5 bolts have three radial lines on the head. Grade 8 bolts have six radial lines. Grade 2 bolts (no markings) should not be used on safety-critical assemblies on a material lift — replace any unmarked bolts you find with at least Grade 5.
